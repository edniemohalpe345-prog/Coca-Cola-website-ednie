History of Coca-Cola

1886: Coca-Cola was founded in Atlanta, Georgia by Dr. John S. Pemberton. The first servings were sold at Jacobs' Pharmacy for 5 cents a glass.

Growth: Coca-Cola has grown into one of the world's most recognized brands and continues to make a difference in communities across 200+ countries.

Legacy: From the iconic contour bottle to "Share a Coke" campaigns, we've always focused on connection, joy, and refreshment.

Mission & Vision

Our Core Values

  • Quality: We deliver the highest standard in every bottle.
  • Inclusion: Everyone is welcome at our table — teens, families, all backgrounds.
  • Innovation: Zero Sugar, plant bottles, and new flavors for modern tastes.
  • Sustainability: World Without Waste initiative — recyclable packaging by 2030.

Target Audiences in Detail

Teenagers (13–19): We connect through TikTok, gaming, and limited-edition drops. Fanta and Sprite are top choices.

Young Adults (20–30): Diet Coke, Coca-Cola Zero Sugar, and smartwater align with health-conscious, active lifestyles.

Families: Classic Coke and multi-packs for gatherings, BBQs, and celebrations. Share a Coke with loved ones.

Milestones & Achievements

  • 1894 – First bottled Coca-Cola.
  • 1915 – Iconic contour bottle design patented.
  • 1985 – "New Coke" and return of Classic formula.
  • 2016 – "One Brand" global strategy launched.
  • 2024 – Over 1.9 billion servings served daily.
